Title: Content Marketing Best Practices for LinkedIn, Twitter, and Blogs
Source: AI Content Studio Knowledge Base

LinkedIn Content Strategy:
LinkedIn posts that perform best follow a specific structure. The first line (the hook) must create enough curiosity to make the reader click "see more". Effective hooks use pattern interrupts — surprising statistics, counterintuitive statements, or direct challenges to conventional wisdom. LinkedIn's algorithm rewards posts that generate dwell time (people reading the full post) and meaningful comments over mere likes.

The optimal LinkedIn post length is 1,200-1,600 characters. Posts should use short paragraphs (1-3 lines maximum) with white space between them, as this improves mobile readability. Bullet points with emoji markers (→, ✅, 🔥) increase scroll-stopping power. Always end with a clear call-to-action or a question to drive comments.

LinkedIn posts about personal experience, lessons learned, and professional failures consistently outperform purely informational content. The platform rewards authenticity. Hashtags (3-5 maximum) should be specific rather than generic — #AIEngineering outperforms #AI.

Twitter/X Thread Strategy:
Twitter threads outperform single tweets by 5-10x in impressions. The first tweet in a thread must be complete and compelling on its own — many users will see only this tweet in their feed. Use numbers in the first tweet ("7 things I learned building an AI system") as they signal value and completeness.

Each tweet in a thread should be self-contained but create anticipation for the next. End tweets with "→" or "(thread)" or a numbered indicator like "2/7" to signal continuation. Threads between 5-12 tweets perform best. Beyond 12, engagement drops sharply.

Twitter rewards specificity. "I reduced API costs by 40% using semantic caching" outperforms "I made my AI system more efficient". Always include the specific method, number, or insight.

Blog Post Structure:
High-performing blog posts follow the AIDA framework: Attention (hook headline), Interest (problem statement), Desire (solution + proof), Action (clear next step). The headline is the most important element — spend 20% of writing time on it.

SEO-optimized blog posts target one primary keyword and 3-5 related keywords. The primary keyword should appear in the title, first paragraph, one H2 heading, and naturally throughout the text. Optimal blog post length for SEO in 2024 is 1,500-2,500 words for informational content.

Use the inverted pyramid structure: most important information first, supporting details after. This serves both readers who skim and search engines that weight early content more heavily. Include a TL;DR summary at the top for scanners.

Subheadings every 300-400 words improve readability and SEO. Each subheading should be descriptive and keyword-rich. Images, code blocks, and examples break up text and increase time-on-page, a key ranking signal.

Engagement Optimization Across Platforms:
The single most important engagement driver is specificity. Vague claims ("AI improves productivity") generate no engagement. Specific claims with proof ("Our AI content pipeline reduced writing time from 4 hours to 12 minutes across 50 content pieces") generate shares, saves, and comments.

Social proof, numbers, and real examples consistently outperform theoretical explanations. "Here's exactly how we did X" outperforms "Here's how you could do X". First-person experience posts outperform second-person advice posts.

Posting consistency matters more than posting frequency. One high-quality post per week outperforms five mediocre daily posts on LinkedIn. On Twitter, daily posting is rewarded by the algorithm, but quality should not be sacrificed.
